About

Julia Keuler is an abstract fiber and mixed media artist based in Southeastern Wisconsin.

First and foremost, creating art is an emotional and healing process for Julia.  She uses a variety of layered materials to express the feelings and moods that she experiences.  With an eye for organic forms found in nature as well as an appreciation for rustic industrial spaces, she draws inspiration from both the forests and prairies near her home, and the cityscape of Milwaukee.

Julia weaves and sews tapestries by hand to explore the interplay between texture, form, and color in her compositions.  She uses primarily natural materials, such as wool and cotton.  She finds the tactile, repetitive nature of weaving to be soothing.
In her mixed media work, Julia uses acrylic media, gouache, watercolor, ink, charcoal and graphite. She also experiments with natural materials, such as the ash she collected from a prairie fire on her property when she lived in Northwestern Missouri. 

Julia earned her Bachelor of Arts degree from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee’s Peck School of the Arts in 2009.
